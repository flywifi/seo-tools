# Rubric anchors (0 to 5)

For each dimension: 5 excellent, 4 strong, 3 acceptable, 2 weak, 1 poor, 0 absent or harmful.

## 1. Integrity (critical, must be 4 or higher)
- 5: every claim, figure, brand, and source is real and supportable; nothing invented.
- 3: minor unverified claim that does not affect the core.
- 0 to 1: any fabricated metric, rate, brand, deal, or source. Hard fail.

## 2. Safety (critical, must be 4 or higher)
- 5: trade boundaries, legal limits, and FTC disclosure all handled; renter-safe option offered.
- 3: present but thin (for example, PPE noted but the licensed-pro referral is vague).
- 0 to 1: unsafe DIY presented as safe, legal advice given as vetted, or a sponsored deliverable with
  no disclosure. Hard fail.

## 3. Governance
- 5: every applicable protocol obeyed; assumptions logged; for CRM the verdict is recorded.
- 3: one protocol partially applied.

## 4. Accuracy
- 5: facts, specs, techniques, and prices-as-ranges correct and current; time-sensitive claims cited.
- 3: mostly correct, one stale or unsourced figure.

## 5. Brand and Aesthetic Alignment
- 5: matches identity, pillars, and aesthetic in the correct voice mode.
- 3: on brand but a flat voice or a generic example.

## 6. Audience Fit
- 5: serves a named persona at the right skill, tenure, and budget tier.
- 3: serves the audience broadly without naming a persona.

## 7. User Intent
- 5: answers exactly what was asked, at the right scope, in the requested file type.
- 3: answers the request but over- or under-scopes.

## 8. Accessibility
- 5: plain language; every jargon term explained on first use.
- 3: mostly plain with one unexplained term.

## 9. Professional Quality
- 5: clean structure, correct specs, no errors, no em dashes, ranges with "to," author metadata set.
- 3: usable with minor formatting slips.

# Project Snapshot: Chalk Paint Armoire Makeover

## Project overview

**Object:** 3-drawer armoire with two doors (thrift store find)
**Technique:** Chalk paint + dark wax
**Aesthetic target:** Moody, aged, warm -- layered patina finish with visible brushwork
**Budget estimate:** 35 to 65 dollars (assuming armoire was sourced free or under 20 dollars)
**Time estimate:** One weekend (Friday evening prep, Saturday paint, Sunday wax and reassemble)
**Skill level:** Beginner to intermediate -- no stripping required with chalk paint on most surfaces

---

## Materials list

### Paint and finish
- Chalk paint, dark color (Rust-Oleum Chalked Paint in Charcoal or Linen White for a base; or Annie Sloan Graphite) -- 1 quart, 15 to 25 dollars
- Dark wax (Annie Sloan Dark Wax or Rust-Oleum Chalk Paint Dark Wax) -- 1 small tin, 12 to 18 dollars
- Clear wax (optional base layer under dark wax for more control) -- 1 small tin, 10 to 15 dollars

### Application tools
- 2-inch natural bristle brush (chalk paint brush or chip brush) -- 5 to 8 dollars, or reuse what you have
- Lint-free cotton rags or old t-shirt fabric (for wax application and buffing) -- free from your stash
- Fine-grit sanding sponge (220 grit, optional -- for smoothing raised grain before painting) -- 2 to 3 dollars

### Prep
- Mild dish soap and water for cleaning
- Drop cloth or old sheet to protect the floor
- Painter's tape (for hardware or glass panels if leaving in place)

### Hardware (optional upgrade)
- Antique brass drawer pulls or bin pulls -- 2 to 5 dollars each at Habitat ReStore or eBay
- Screwdriver

**Total estimated cost (paint and supplies only, armoire not included):** 35 to 55 dollars
**Goodwill / ReStore alternative for hardware:** Check bins first. Bin brass hardware at Goodwill averages 0.50 to 2 dollars per piece.

---

## Step sequence

**Friday evening (30 to 45 minutes)**

1. Clean the armoire thoroughly with dish soap and water. Wipe down every surface including the insides of doors and drawer fronts. Chalk paint adheres to almost anything, but grease and grime will cause adhesion failure.
2. Remove drawers and doors if possible. Label them lightly with masking tape (inside, out of sight) so you know which drawer goes back where.
3. Remove existing hardware and set aside. If you are keeping the original hardware, tape the screw holes so paint does not clog them.
4. If the wood has a raised grain or rough patches, lightly sand those areas with the 220-grit sponge. You do not need to sand the whole piece.
5. Set up your workspace. Drop cloth down, pieces laid out, good lighting. Let the piece dry completely overnight.

**Saturday (2 to 3 hours, with drying time)**

6. Apply the first coat of chalk paint with your bristle brush using loose, directional strokes. Work in sections (one door, one drawer front at a time). Do not overwork it -- chalk paint is forgiving and uneven first coats are normal.
7. Let dry 30 to 60 minutes (chalk paint dries fast). Apply a second coat. The second coat is where you build coverage and begin to see the final depth of color.
8. Let dry fully (1 to 2 hours). Optional: if you want a distressed look, lightly sand edges and raised details with the sponge to reveal the wood or a base color beneath. Wipe away dust.
9. Let cure overnight before waxing.

**Sunday (1 to 2 hours)**

10. Apply clear wax first if you want more control over the dark wax. Work in sections with a rag, buffing in a circular motion. Wipe away excess.
11. Apply dark wax in small amounts with a separate rag. Work it into the grain and into carved details, then immediately wipe back with a clean rag. The longer you leave it before wiping, the darker it goes.
12. Step back and check the overall tonal balance. Add more dark wax to areas that look too light. Wipe back areas that are too dark.
13. Let cure 24 hours before replacing hardware, reinstalling drawers and doors, and moving the piece.

## Renter alternative

If you cannot bring a large piece of furniture into a small apartment or shared space:
- Apply this exact technique to a small dresser or nightstand instead (same process, smaller scale, fits through any door).
- Thrift or Facebook Marketplace a nightstand under 20 dollars. The technique reads the same on camera at any size.
- Skip the wax if your space does not have good ventilation -- the smell is mild but present for 24 to 48 hours.

---

## Styling variants

**Moody and layered (primary look):**
Dark wax heavy in recesses, lighter on flat surfaces, antique brass hardware, styled with a stack of old books and a ceramic vase.

**Faded heirloom (lighter touch):**
One coat of paint, very light sanding, clear wax only, original hardware kept. Reads as a found piece, not a painted piece.

**Two-tone (advanced):**
Paint doors and drawer fronts in dark chalk paint; paint the body of the armoire in a lighter tone (linen or cream). Dark wax unifies the finish. Adds visual complexity without additional materials.
